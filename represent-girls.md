## Neurodivergent Girls are often overlooked and misunderstood.

As the mothers of daughters, this is a new and developing area for RepreSENt. One issue we have persistently come up against is that the needs of girls are often overlooked in both mainstream and specialist provision.

We feel this is because girls can be better at masking their needs than boys, whose needs may be more visible. This is clear in the different diagnosis levels between boys and girls for autism and ADHD, and in the gender disparities seen in specialist provision.

We have met with the head of [Limpsfield Grange](https://limpsfieldgrange.co.uk/), the only school for autistic girls in the country, and this strengthened our commitment to ensuring the needs of neurodivergent girls are not overlooked in any provision we develop.

We are particularly interested in the idea of an internalised presentation of autism. We follow and signpost to resources from organisations and groups including SASS, the [Autistic Girls Network](https://autisticgirlsnetwork.org/) and [Girls Have Autism Too](https://www.girlshaveautismtoo.org.uk/).

Alice has started to collate resources for neurodivergent girls, including resources recommended by neurodivergent girls she works with. This will be a work in progress for a while.
