# Founders

## Co-Founder Alice Hoyle

I am a late diagnosed neurodivergent mum of three girls. Needs in my family include Autism, ADHD, dyslexia, dyscalculia and sensory processing needs with other associated needs.

I work nationally, and sometimes internationally, as an educational consultant. I specialise in Personal, Social and Health Education (PSHE), with a particular focus on Relationships, Sex and Health Education (RSHE). I train teachers, work with parents, and teach students directly.

I have worked as a teacher, head of department, local authority adviser and national specialist teacher. I am also passionate about sensory wellbeing and the importance of education providers meeting sensory needs.

I co-founded RepreSENt during the period my middle child was undergoing diagnosis and we realised how much mainstream education had missed her needs. Emma Bolt offered invaluable support during the EHCP application process and, after many cups of tea and ranting sessions, we decided we would set up a school ourselves if we could not find suitable secondary schools for our daughters.

Emma now takes on the day-to-day lead on RepreSENt as she has more capacity and is far more strategic and organised than my own brand of ADHD squirrel sidequests. We make an awesome team.

You can find out more about me at <a href="https://www.alicehoyle.com/">alicehoyle.com</a>.

Contact Alice if:

- You are looking for support with how to talk to your child about SEND-friendly Relationships and Sex Education.
- You want support with your child's education provider becoming sensory aware.
- You are looking for a mentor or coach for your neurodivergent child exploring life skills, particularly sensory advocacy.
- You want to get involved with RepreSENt.

## Co-Founder Emma Bolt

Emma’s professional background spans sales, sponsorship, marketing, communications and higher education. Since returning to the UK, she has worked in professional services roles in Higher Education, most recently establishing and running an Immigration and International Student Advice Service, with a focus on improving student experience and engagement.

Becoming a mum has been the most all-consuming, empowering and challenging role she has had. Her eldest presents with a full range of needs, including ADD, DCD, APD, dyslexia, dyscalculia, speech and language and sensory processing needs. Her youngest is dyslexic.

Emma’s professional expertise and personal dedication keep her focused on pushing for positive changes within the education system, highlighting the need for wider educational options and SEND advocacy.

Contact Emma if:

- You are looking for support with an EHCP application or need signposting and a handhold with a new diagnosis.
- You are a SEND teacher interested in teaching at a new school.
